## Executive Summary

TeeAhead enters a content landscape dominated by GolfNow (massive blog + help center), Club Caddie (active operator-focused blog), and aggregators like Supreme Golf. TeeAhead's 7 existing pages provide a credible but thin content footprint — strong on tools and landing pages, completely absent on education, local, and GEO/AI-citation content.

The highest-leverage opportunity is **anti-GolfNow operator education**: the market is actively searching for "how much does GolfNow cost," "cancel GolfNow contract," and "GolfNow alternatives" at volumes TeeAhead can realistically rank for (KD 7–24) within 6–12 months from a zero-DR start. Over 100 golf courses dropped GolfNow in early 2025; that dissatisfaction is a live search signal.

The second major opportunity is **Metro Detroit local SEO**: ~150 public courses in 7 counties, 1,200–1,800 monthly searches for "golf courses Metro Detroit," and GolfNow's only local content is a 2017 blog post. A fresh, comprehensive local guide would win this on recency and specificity alone.

**Three content pillars to build:**
1. **GolfNow exposé cluster** — operator education, barter math, contract escape, SEO theft explainers
2. **Metro Detroit golf hub** — local course guides, seasonal content, golfer community
3. **Software education cluster** — tee sheet buying guides, loyalty program how-tos, data ownership explainers
